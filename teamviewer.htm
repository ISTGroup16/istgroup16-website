Remote Access: Team Viewer Software

What is Team Viewer?

Team Viewer is an all-in-one solution for remote support, remote access, and online meetings. In 2005, a small company based in Southern Germany invented this software to connect to their customers' computers and showcase new applications remotely. The software allows for effective communication at any time, and is perfect for online meetings as there is quick and easy exchange.

Today, Team Viewer helps around 630,000 customers worldwide and from all industries to digitalize their business-critical processes. The companies using this software are most often found in the United States and in the Information Technology and Services industry. They often have an average of one million dollars in revenue. The British Petroleum Company p.l.c is an example of a company that uses Team Viewer software. It is a multinational oil, gas and chemicals organisation that is based in the United Kingdom.

To add on, Team Viewer connects users from anywhere in the world, with whatever device they are using. The software is compatible with the following types of devices: Androids, iOS, mobile, tablet, desktop, and laptop. On this page, you will learn how to set up your devices as well as how to connect to remote devices and users.

How Can One Use Team Viewer?

The first thing to do is type in "teamviewer login" on your device browser. From there you can either sign in if you already have an account, or sign up if you do not. The following is a link to the Team Viewer login page:

This is Team viewer's login page.

The next step is to go back to the main Team Viewer page, click the "Free Download" button at the right corner of the screen. From there, you will download the files appropriate for what you want to use Team Viewer for.

- The TeamViewer Host app is used for 24/7 access to remote computers, making it an ideal solution for remote monitoring, server maintenance, or home-office access.

- The TeamViewer Quick Support app enables incoming remote control connections but it does not allow the start of outgoing connections.
